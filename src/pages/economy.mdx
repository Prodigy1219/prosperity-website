## Philosophy

The economy is designed around **player trade**. The admin shop exists as a fallback, not as the main market. High-end items come from players, exploration, crafting, quests, contracts, or auctions.

If you want an elytra, you buy it from a player. If you want high-tier tools, shulker boxes, or rare materials — you find a player who has them.

<InfoCallout type="info" title="Economy Balance Notice">
  New Prosperity is under construction. Economy values, prices, rewards, and sink rates may be adjusted before and after launch based on observed inflation, deflation, and player feedback.
</InfoCallout>

## Money Faucets

Money enters the economy through controlled channels:

- **Jobs** — Regular payment for work done in your profession
- **Public contracts** — Completing development contracts for the city
- **Quests** — One-time or repeatable task quests
- **Admin shop sales** — Selling basic resources at controlled rates
- **Auctions** — Bidding on rare server-distributed items

## Money Sinks

Money leaves the economy through:

- **Plot and land purchases** — Buying land from the city
- **Licenses** — Required for advanced activities (vehicles, companies, industrial operations)
- **Upkeep costs** — Machinery and vehicles cost money to maintain
- **Fines** — In-game law violations result in fines paid to the city or victims
- **Taxes** — Plot taxes paid periodically

## Admin Shop Limits

The admin shop is deliberately limited:

- **Does not sell**: Elytras, shulker boxes, totems of undying, high-tier tools, vehicles, or rare enchantment books
- **Does sell**: Basic materials at capped daily quantities to prevent abuse
- **Buying**: Purchases basic resources at controlled rates with daily limits

The admin shop is a pressure valve, not a market.

## Player Shops

Player shops are the real economy:

- Any player with a **Merchant license** can open a shop
- Shops are located in commercial plots in the market district
- Shops set their own prices — supply and demand drive the market
- Player shops should consistently beat admin shop prices on common goods

## Land as Wealth Progression

Land is one of the most important assets on the server:

| Plot Type | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Starter plots** | Small residential plots near spawn, affordable for new players |
| **Commercial plots** | Market district shops, used for selling goods |
| **Industrial land** | Factories, farms, and production systems |
| **Development plots** | Unlocked through community funding over time |

Owning land is a milestone — it is not just a home, it is a store of value.

## Licenses

Licenses are required for advanced activities:

| License | Required For |
|---|---|
| Merchant | Opening a player shop |
| Industrialist | Operating machinery and semi-auto production |
| Vehicle Operator | Driving wheeled or aerial vehicles |
| Builder | Taking on large public construction contracts |
| Company Formation | Starting a company with employees |

Licenses cost money and may require skill tree progress to unlock.

## Public Contracts

Public contracts bridge player productivity and world development:

- The city posts contracts for needed resources: timber, stone, food, materials
- Players complete contracts and are paid by the city treasury
- Resources are consumed and used to build public infrastructure
- This is the primary intended money faucet for active players

## Inflation Control

The server uses several mechanisms to control inflation:

- Daily limits on admin shop buying prices
- Periodic balance reviews — staff review median player wealth monthly
- Sink rate adjustments — license prices and taxes may rise if inflation is detected
- Limited money faucets — contracts and jobs are gated by time or quantity
