## Philosophy

The economy is designed around **player trade**. The admin shop exists as a fallback, not as the main market. High-end items come from players — elytras, high-tier tools, rare materials, enchanted gear.

If you want an elytra, you buy it from a player. If you want a Fortune IV pickaxe, you find someone who built the progression to make one.

## How Income Actually Works

Your hourly income is not just your job wage — it's the sum of four sources:

| Source | Description |
|---|---|
| **Job wage** | Direct cash from Jobs Reborn when you take an action (mine a block, harvest a crop, kill a mob) |
| **Admin item floor** | Value of your drops sold to the admin shop — the safe fallback, not the real market |
| **Player market upside** | Extra value from selling to other players — this is the real economy |
| **Contracts** | Controlled bonus payouts from public contracts, civic commissions, and events |

The job wage is a labor subsidy — it's intentionally modest. The real income comes from what you do with the resources you collect.

**Target effective income by stage:**

| Stage | Target $/hr |
|---|---|
| New player (level 1) | $125–$175 |
| Mid-level (around level 25) | $175–$225 |
| Level 50 maxed job | $225–$275 |
| Established multi-job player | $300–$600 |
| Late-game (drills, Eff VII, contracts) | $600–$1,500+ |

## The Three-Price Rule

Every important item has three prices:

```
Admin buy price  <  Player market price  <  Admin sell price
```

- **Admin buy** — The server buys at 40–60% of player market value. It's a floor, not the real price.
- **Player market** — The true economy value, set by supply and demand.
- **Admin sell** — If the server sells it at all, it's at 150–300% of player market. A convenience tax, not the standard price.

If admin buy prices are too close to player market prices, players stop trading with each other and just dump everything to the server. The spread is intentional.

## Money Faucets

Money enters the economy through controlled channels:

- **Jobs** — Regular wage paid per manual action in your profession
- **Public contracts** — The city posts contracts for needed resources and construction
- **Starter quests** — One-time intro rewards for new players
- **Admin shop sales** — Selling basic raw materials at floor prices

## Money Sinks

Money leaves the economy through:

- **Plot and land purchases** — Buying plots from the city
- **Plot rental** — Regular weekly fees for rented land and stalls
- **Tools and vehicles** — Drills and tractors purchased with in-game money
- **Fines** — Law violations result in fines paid to the city or victims
- **Company registration** — Fee to form a registered company
- **Development project buy-in** — Fund city expansion and infrastructure

## Jobs and Progression

Jobs are the core progression system. Join up to 3 jobs, level each to 50, and unlock perks through the skill tree as you go.

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| Max jobs at once | 3 (4th slot available for $10,000) |
| Max level per job | 50 |
| Income bonus | +1% per level, max +50% at level 50 |

As you level up, your job title advances — Novice → Apprentice → Elite → Master → Grand Master → Legendary. Open the skill tree with `/skills` to spend level-up points on passive perks.

**Late-game unlocks** (purchased with in-game money, no level gating beyond what's needed to reach them):
- Mining drills (D1/D2/D3) — buy via `/drillShop`
- Farming tractors (Yellow/Red/Green) — buy via `/tractorShop`
- Job slot 4 — $10,000 career expansion permit

## Admin Shop Rules

The admin shop is a **price floor**, not a store. It exists so players always have a guaranteed fallback buyer.

**The server buys:** Basic raw materials — stone, logs, basic crops, raw ores, coal, wool — at floor prices.

**The server sells (fallback only, expensive):** Basic tools, food, torches, seeds.

**The server never sells:** Diamonds, netherite, enchanted items, rare ores, elytra, rare drops. Let the player market set those prices.

## Player Shops

Player shops are the real economy:

- Any player can open a shop in the market district
- Shops are located in commercial plots in the market district
- Shops set their own prices — supply and demand drive the market
- Player shops should consistently beat admin shop prices on common goods

## Land as Wealth

Land is one of the most important assets on the server:

| Plot Type | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Starter plots** | Small residential plots near spawn, affordable for new players |
| **Commercial plots** | Market district shops, used for selling goods |
| **Industrial land** | Farms, production systems, machinery |
| **Development plots** | Unlocked through community funding over time |

Owning land is a milestone. It is not just a home — it is a store of value and a business foundation.

## Public Contracts

Public contracts bridge player productivity and world development:

- The city posts contracts for needed resources: timber, stone, food, materials
- Players complete contracts and are paid by the city treasury
- Resources are consumed to build public infrastructure
- This is the primary intended money faucet for active, organized players

## Inflation Control

- Jobs wages are tested monthly and adjusted if combined income is too high or too low
- Admin shop buy prices stay well below player market prices
- Plot prices and the 4th-job-slot permit scale as sinks for accumulated wealth
- Late-game sinks (development projects, company registration, Efficiency VII path) absorb large amounts of money
